The E-Waste Crisis

Understanding the environmental challenge of traditional computing

Mounting E-Waste

Global e-waste reached 57 million tons in 2023, growing at 3-5% annually. Less than 20% is properly recycled, with the rest ending in landfills or being informally processed in developing countries.

Environmental Impact: Toxic materials like lead, mercury, and cadmium leach into soil and water, contaminating ecosystems and food chains. Valuable resources like gold, silver, and rare earth metals are lost.

Energy Inefficiency

Data centers consume 3% of global electricity, projected to reach 8% by 2030. Traditional processors waste 60-70% of energy as heat, requiring extensive cooling systems that further increase energy consumption.

Carbon Footprint: The ICT sector accounts for 2-3% of global CO2 emissions - equivalent to the aviation industry. This is projected to double by 2025 without intervention.

Resource-Intensive Manufacturing

Producing a single computer chip requires 32kg of water and 1.6kg of fossil fuels. Chip manufacturing accounts for 30% of electronics' total carbon footprint due to energy-intensive processes.

Resource Depletion: Mining for rare earth metals causes deforestation, water pollution, and habitat destruction. Many critical materials face supply chain vulnerabilities.
